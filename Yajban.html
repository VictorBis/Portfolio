What is Yaj ban?

An enterprise that makes regional dresses of Mexico

Yaj ban is a small enterprise from Mexico City, founded by an Oaxacan seamstress, which has been making regional dresses for almost 20 years and wants to get into the digital world. Its main goal is to keep alive the traditions and clothes that make Mexico so unique.

The Problem

Dancers and dance teachers need to buy regional dresses in a faster way because going to the physical stores is so time-consuming

People who want to buy a dress still need to go to the markets and look for the right ones, that might take a lot of time, prices are high and now, due to COVID-19, they’ll need to go to specific hours and specific days to be able to get inside.

User Personas

Defining personas

Based on my research, I realized that two main users will be benefited.

I defined 2 personas, the first one is a dance teacher, who is looking for a dress with high-quality materials and which design fits his necessities. The other one is a dancer, who wants to buy the best dress without spending too much money.

Competitor Analysis

Almost everyone sells on social networks

In order to identify what’s already on the market, what others did well and how can I improve their solutions to make a better product, I researched with 3 direct competitors. It was hard to find competitors that aren’t only on social media, but in the end, I found a site that helped me to make a more complete research.

The Solution

A website that allows people to get dresses online

The website shows the catalog of dresses available and all the information about it. People can order what they need at any time, and they have the option to try it on in one of the physical stores before they buy it.

Mind Map

Ideating features and organization of the website

Key Features

Based on the research, I defined the features that the website must have

  • Detailed and clear product information. Users need to know as much as they can about the dress, as they can’t either see it or touch it until they receive it.
  • Product search. It helps to easily find products.
  • Book an appointment Some users need to try on the dress before they buy it.
  • Customer Support. Fast and efficient communication with the user.
  • Efficient checkout process. Users need an easy and fast way to purchase a product, some of them have never bought online, so, the website must allow multiple payment options and makes them feel secure.

Logo and Name

Before starting to design the website, I needed to define the name and logo of the brand

Name

The enterprise was born in a town called Villa Hidalgo Yalalag, Oaxaca, where people speak Zapoteco. So I proposed to name the brand with a word in this language.

The pronunciation and writing of some words are very difficult for non-speakers, but in the end, we found the word that matches what we were looking for.

Yaj ban means 'Living flower'. The flower is a very representative element of the Yalalg's culture, and it's used to create clothes, the most important component of a dress.

Logo

I presented logos with threads, sewing machines, needles, and some elements used in the fabrication of a dress, but we decided to match the name with the logo. The best way to do it was using the Huipil Yalalteco, the traditional dress of Yalalg.

The Final Design

The website

Home

I kept the home page clean, only showing the essential. I wanted to demonstrate how easy is to get a dress, so I decided to place it in the first section. In the interviews I realized that some users want to know that the people they're dealing with are reliable, that's why I decided to create two sections, one dedicated to showing who we are, displaying the photo of the seamstress, and demonstrating all the expertise we have in the industry, and the second one, which shows testimonials of people who have worked with us.

Products List

I maintained the product list similar to a lot of e-commerce websites and displaying the most important information. I wanted to facilitate the way users can find the dress they're looking for, so I added a search box in the navigation bar and placed a dropdown that lets them sort the products by name or price.

Single Product

On the single product page, I only colocated the information people wanted. Name, price, a brief description that includes the materials, colors available at the moment, sizes and its size chart, and the options to buy now or book an appointment. It was important to show that we have more options than the ones displayed, but they may not be available at the moment, that's why I placed the contact us option at the bottom of the section.

Purchase

The buy now, book an appointment, and contact us page are very similar. It was hard to maintain all the process inside the website due to different factors, but it allowed me to create a really short and easy form. In order to avoid confusion, I wrote a brief description that indicates what the users need to do and that we are going to contact them once we receive the message.

Test

I'm currently working on the test to improve the website

I wanted to do the first test with the wireframes, but it's been hard to find people who are willing to do live testing and some of them didn't understand it at all. Fortunately, I have the emails of the people who helped me with the interviews and they might be willing to help.

I have a group of people who will help me to answer a survey, so I created some scenarios.

  1. You want to buy a Tehuana dress for your daughter and she has a 73 cm waist. Find the right size and purchase it.
  2. You want to try on a red and size 24 Aguascalientes dress. Select the right dress and book an appointment on Friday 12 at 10 am.
  3. You want to know what determines the shipping rate but you don't want to contact us. The answer is already on the website, so write what determines the shipping rate.

Why?

I made some important decisions that I think need to be clarified

  • Why the user can’t buy inside the website?
    The main reason is that we can’t calculate the shipping rate automatically, it depends on many factors, so we can’t show an exact final price. Displaying only the price of the dress and letting users pay it without knowing the shipping rate would cause a lot of problems.
  • Why the user can’t book an appointment inside the website?
    The store might be far away from the user’s house, so we give them the option to meet us at an intermediate point. Defining this point may take several messages that can’t be hosted on the website, so we decided to make all this process in an app that is more comfortable for the user and seller.

What I learned

Working on a low budget project

With this project, I learned to do things I thought I wouldn't do but helped me to acquire new skills.

  • Finding users to interview was really hard, and it got more complicated because the main market in Mexico City to find potential users is closed due to COVID-19. I sent messages to more than 30 Instagram and Facebook accounts and only 12 answered me. When the firsts answers were coming, I realized that I had to add and be more specific in the questions I was asking in the survey, fortunately, an online survey is really easy to update.

    I had the chance to do some 1:1 interviews and I realized that is so much different than doing online surveys.
  • The enterprise didn't have any photos of the dresses, so I had to take and edit all of them.
  • Defining a name for an enterprise that matches with the values and work an enterprise does is really hard, also a lot of domain names are already taken, which makes it even more complicated. Luckily the enterprise had unique roots that facilitated this process so much.
  • Before doing this project I built some school projects, but I've never considered interviewing users to understand their necessities. I had an idea of how the website was going to look like, but a lot of things I had in mind wasn't exactly what the users wanted.
