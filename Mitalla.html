Context

Everything started when my mom bought a dress that didn't fit her

This is a personal project that I wanted to develop in order to find a solution for a problem my social circle, and a lot of people, has had in the last months due to the way we have to acquire clothes nowadays, and which is something new for some of us.

The Problem

Have you ever bought clothes online that didn't fit you?

COVID-19 changed the way we used to do a lot of things, buying clothes is one of them. Physical stores had to close for so long and people had to buy clothes online, something that some of them had never done. Unfortunately, some people had a bad experience buying something that didn't fit them, even though they've bought in the store before or used the size chart to select the right one.

Sizes vary on different clothes despite they are from the same store, maybe it's because its materials or its manufacture, but there's something that makes them different.

The Solution

An app that helps people choose the right size for their body

The app is based on the information people give about the clothes they've bought, letting us know how it fit them and its real measures.

People introduce all the information possible about its body measures (height, weight, chest, waist, etc.) and, with the database generated with the information we obtained, we'll give them the size that best fits them in the item they want to buy.

User Interviews

I conducted 8 interviews with people who recently have returned what they bought online

From the people I interviewed, 5 of them had never bought online, but they've already acquired items in the physical store of the brand before. The rest of them where people who have already bought online and had their first or second bad experience.

What went wrong?

  • The website didn't have a size chart or it wasn't visible.
  • Their measures were in the limit of two sizes, so they weren't sure which size should they buy.
  • They've bought clothes of the brand before, but the size they selected didn't fit the same as the clothes they had.
  • They followed the size chart, but they received an item that didn't follow the specifications.
  • The customer support of the store never answered their questions or didn't help as they wished.
  • They didn't like how it looked on them.

Competitor Analysis

What's the solution the stores have in their websites?

Almost everyone tried to solve the problem with a size chart, which isn't the best solution at all, as their clothes may vary in their actual sizes. People can go to the comments and read what other people experienced, but comments are sorted by its rating, which implies that comments from people who had the best experiences are at the top, so the ones that might help with this situation tend to get lost.

Brainstorming

Ideating possible solutions to solve the problem

Key Features

Features the app must have

  • Used as fewer times as possible. Size charts are different in every store, so they need to measure their body each time. This app needs to speed up the process of buying an item in order to not hinder it.
  • Search Bar. There must be a way to find the item you want to buy and the size that fits you inside the app.
  • Testimonials. Some people I interviewed don't trust size charts anymore, and decided not to buy clothes until the physical stores open. It can be solved by doing right recommendations and letting people know what other users experienced.
  • Help in app. Measuring your body could be a little confusing for some people, they're not really sure if they're doing it right, and some websites don't have images to make this process easier.

Sitemap

The structure of the app

I decided to keep just two main sections, as they're the primary actions of the app. The Home section will display mainly the search bar and some recommendations of clothes. The profile is a section that will be used to log in or to update your information. The other main action is adding an item, and I decided to give it one whole section.

User Flow

There are two main actions that a user can do in the app

The first one is trying to find your size of an item you want to buy, and the second one is adding an item to the app in order to help people to find the best size that fits them in an item you've bought.

Design System

The Final Design

My solution

Home

The home section contains all the free content that any user can explore. With its horizontal scrollable menu users can explore through the categories of the information and the filter option lets them explore by genres (biography, crime, fiction, etc).

Children

The children section has the same structure as the home section, they'll differentiate each other by the content each one displays.

Search

There's a lot of content available, so the search section will help the users to find the information they're looking for. I decided to add suggestions behind the search bar in order to give more options, we can't display smart suggestions depending on the user's behavior, so the suggestions will be defined by the necessities and goals of the association at the moment.

Gallery

The association needed to show all the media they have, this section is similar as other apps shows its media. At the moment, due to the way the content is organized, it's not possible to add a menu that can helps to explore content by category, but it might be an option that can be added in an update, depending on the feedback we'll get.

Enterprise

Enterprises are the ones that keep alive this project, so they receive exclusive content depending on the goals they have. The login form just asks for the code we give the employees of the enterprises that work with us. If someone forgets the code, there's no way to get it inside the app due to we don't have the tools to automatize it at the moment.

Enterprises get new employees and loses some constantly, with the structure we have there's no way to deny the access to an employee that doesn't work for an enterprise anymore, that's why I decided to change the code every month, so we can keep restrung the access.

item

The main content of the app are items. A lot of our publications in social networks are shared, that's why I decided to add this option inside the app. In order to give the item more space, I decided to remove the tab bar when the users scroll down, and it'll appears when they scroll up.

We received comments of some people who told us that a problem they had is that when they're reading a large item, they sometimes forget the title or the main subject, that's why I decided to always show what the user is reading.

Test

I defined some scenarios which include features that I want to test

Due to COVID-19, the test we had planned couldn't be done, but we are going to make remote tests with some users that constantly participate in the programs of the association.

  1. A parent of one of your students wants to find a fiction book for his kid but you're out of ideas. Look for one book that fits the necessities mentioned.
  2. You saw one of our items on Facebook but you didn't have the chance to read it at the moment. Fortunately you remember that the title is "El arte detrás del teatro". Find the item inside the app, you're free to use any feature you think will help you.
  3. You work for BBVA, the code of the enterprise is ABC123. Access to the content for this enterprise and look for the last reading challenge.
  4. You saw the poster of one of our "Bibliopola" conference and you know that one of your friends can't miss it. Share the image with your friend via e-mail.

What I learned

Designing an app that has users waiting for its launch

With this project I learned to do things I though I wouldn't do, but helped me to acquire new skills.

  • This project had already an organization of the information, so I had to adapt everything I had in mind to fit with the organization.
  • I'm going to develop this app, so I had to design it considering my skills and the time it will take me to implement each feature.
  • Working with people who aren't involved in this field is a little hard, sometimes the communication is a problem, but the way we can complement our ideas is amazing.
  • Working completely remotely is a new experience, but I liked the way we can work and it helped me to be more responsible.
