What is Cinépolis?

The biggest cinema in Mexico and Latin America, and one of the companies that sells more tickets in the world

I love Cinépolis, almost everyone I know prefer it over other companies because of the quality and service they provide, but its app isn't what people want and need.

The Problem

Cinépoli’s users prefer going to the cinema and buy their tickets there than using the app because its performance is really poor

People don't use the Cinépolis app because it doesn't work as people expected, the reviews in the App Store are full of complaints. Its purpose is to speed up the process of getting tickets and food, but people still prefer to go to the ticket office.

User Interviews

I interviewed 4 people who are frequent clients of Cinépolis

From the people I interviewed, two are in their 40s and the other two in their 20s. My goal was to know their experience with the app and how they use it.

This is what I found:

  • They go to Cinépolis at least once a month, but none of them has bought tickets with the app in the most recent ones.
  • All of them have downloaded the app, but just one used it to buy tickets.
  • They don't use it because it's almost impossible to use.
  • One of them uninstalled the app.
  • They don't recommend the app, two of them don't use it anymore, and the other one just use it to see the schedules of the movies.
  • If they buy tickets online, they buy them on the website.

User Reviews

I complemented the information I got with the reviews of the users in the App Store

A lot of people has used the app, they have over 10k reviews, but unfortunately the comments aren't the best ones.

Usability Test

I did a usability test to see how users interact with the app and to identify more pain points

I did the test with the people I interviewed, asking them to do the most common tasks a user can do in the app.

I defined the next tasks:

  • Buy a ticket for the movie "Amores Perros".
  • Change your location to see the schedule of movies in the cinemas located downtown.
  • Explore the catalog of presales.
  • Check your level and how many points you have.

Pain Ponts

The main problem is in the development side, but it still has problems that can be solved before implementation

With the analysis I did at the beginning, the interviews, reviews and the usability test, I confirmed that the app has a lot of problems that can be solved only with code, and it's the main reason why the app isn't used.

I found some pain points:

  • Can't explore the cinemas out of your town.
    Users want to explore the schedule of cinemas that are outside their town, but they can't do it.
  • The process of buying a ticket is large.
    Users didn't like to introduce all their data in the process, because they've already introduced it when they logged in, and felt that the process is needlessly large.
  • The button "Siguiente" isn't visible.
    In the process of buying a ticket, some users didn't notice the next button.
  • Selecting the seats is confusing.
    When you go to the cinema they always indicate you where is the screen, surprisingly this is really necessary, as some users that helped me with the test dind't notice it.
  • After you buy a ticket, there's no confirmation.
    I found two comments in the App Store that mentioned that when you finish your purchase, the app doesn't show you a confirmation.
  • Can't add or update a credit card.
    Users couldn't add or update a credit card as they can do it in other apps, unless they do it in the process of buying a ticket. This form was complicated for two people that participated in my test.
  • Have to explore all the catalog to find a movie.
    The only way of finding a movie is scrolling through all the content.
  • They ask you for a PIN to see your club information.
    In order to see how many points, visits and all your club information, you need a PIN that some people don't know.
  • The app loads slowly and sometimes freezes.
    When you open the app, sometimes it takes so much time to load all the content, it's common that it freezes and you can't do anything.

The Final Design

The redesign of the app

Home

The home section didn't present many problems, people could navigate through it without problems. I changed the position of the list icon, as people didn't notice it in the test I did. The main change is the search icon, a feature that lets people find a movie faster.

The tab bar now has only two options because the cinema it's already in the home section and there's no necessity to put it there.

Submenus

The submenus have few options, they placed the options at the top and there's a lot of white space, I decided to reduce the size of the container in order to make it easier to reach each option.

Selecting a cinema was a problem for the people because they couldn't see the schedule of movies out of their town. I changed the way people can select the cinemas, allowing them to explore every cinema they want.

Search

People complained because they had to explore through all the catalog to find a movie, it sometimes isn't available and it's a waste of time. The search section displays the cinemas where the movie is available, giving people the chance to explore the main information about the movie they're looking for.

Movie

The movie section only changed in the alignment of the text, the last design had the text justified, which now is left aligned.

Tickets

People didn't notice the next button, it was at the top right of the screen. I changed it to the bottom, keeping the reading flow. Users can introduce a promotional code, this button was at the bottom, I decided to change its place after the ticket selector, as a lot of websites place this field.

Seats

The problem with this section was that people didn't notice the screen, and this made the process of selecting seats confusing. I did the screen bigger, so people can notice it without problems. The seats icons now have more space, so it's easier to select the ones you want without problems.

Payment Process

When you buy a ticket, you have to introduce your data every time, this is something that annoyed users of the app. As the app already has all this data, I decided to ask just for the payment method.

Summary

The summary was on the seats section, as I changed it, I decided to make this a single section at the end of the process, giving people all the information they introduced and a way to go back.

People didn't know if when they purchased a ticket, the process was done successfully, that's why I decided to display a confirmation message after a successful or an error of a purchase.

Profile

The profile section didn't change so much, I removed some options that people I tested the app didn't even notice and were unnecessary.

The purchases section now shows more information about the movie, and the QR code is bigger and has fewer things around it.

Add Card

People didn't have the chance to add a card, the only way to do it was in the process of buying a ticket, so I added this option in My cards section.

Some people had some problems with the add card form, they couldn't find some fields in their card and there was no help. The first change I made was removing the last name field, as Cinépolis does on their website, and adding icons of help on the fields people got confused. The help icon will show an image of a card, highlighting the place where the field is and a description.

What I learned

Don't change what works

  • The app has things that at the beginning I wanted to change, but it turned out that people didn't have problems with them.
  • Code is really important, the main problem of the app is in this part, so design and development need to work well. If one doesn't work, it doesn't matter how well the other is done, people won't use it.
  • Cinépolis has its design system, so I had to search the colors and everything they use and try to maintain as much as possible with what I had.
