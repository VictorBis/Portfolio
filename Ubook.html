Design challenge

Your school wants to provide dedicated spaces for students to help them focus on their academic studies. Design an experience for students, professors, and members of the school community to reserve work desks/spaces in the library

The Problem

Students and professors struggle to find spaces to focus on their studies because the ones at school don't fit their necessities

The library at school is a place where a lot of the school community go to study, but they sometimes face some problems that makes them decide to go somewhere else

Surveys

I conducted 8 interviews with people who recently have returned what they bought online

From the people I interviewed, 2 of them had never bought online, the rest of them were people who have already bought online and had their first or second bad experience.

What went wrong?

  • The website didn't have a size chart, or it wasn't visible.
  • Their measures were between two sizes, so they weren't sure which size should they buy.
  • They've bought clothes of the brand before, but the size they selected didn't fit the same as the clothes they had.
  • They followed the size chart, but they received an item that didn't follow the specifications.
  • The customer support of the store never answered their questions or didn't help as they wished.
  • They didn't like how it looked on them.

What people experienced

User Persona

Meet Cristina

Cristina reflects the user that will be benefited, she's a person who recently bought a sweater that didn't fit her, so she wants to help other people to not make the same mistake. Fortunately, she's not discouraged, and she wants to buy a dress in the same store, but this time she wants to be sure that it fits her perfectly.

Competitor Analysis

What's the solution the stores have on their websites?...
A size chart

Almost everyone tried to solve the problem with a size chart, which isn't the best solution at all, as their clothes may vary in their actual sizes. People can go to the comments and read what other people experienced, but comments are sorted by their rating, which implies that comments from people who had the best experiences are at the top, so the ones that might help with this situation tend to get lost.

Brainstorming

How might we...

I conducted a brainstorming and, in order to open up the session, I defined some How might we questions, such as ...help people find the correct size of their clothes?, ...make the process faster?, ...build trust?, ...make it profitable?, therefore all together provide different potential solutions to the problem.

The Solution

An app that tells people their correct size based on their measures, the item they want to buy and the information of other users

The app is based on the information people give about the clothes they've bought, letting us know how it fit them and its real measures.

People introduce all the information possible about its body measures (height, weight, chest, waist, etc.) and, with the database generated with the information we obtained, we'll give them the size that best fits them in the item they want to buy.

Key Features

Features the app must have

  • Profile. In order to speed up the process, a profile with actual measures will help to give the recommendations fast, and users will measure themselves just one time.
  • Search Bar. There must be a way to find the item you want to buy and the size that fits you inside the app.
  • Testimonials. Some people I interviewed don't trust size charts anymore and decided not to buy clothes until the physical stores open. It can be solved by doing the right recommendations and letting people know what other users experienced.
  • Help in app. Measuring your body could be a little confusing for some people, they're not really sure if they're doing it right, and some websites don't have images to make this process easier.
  • Redirect to stores. To continue the flow of buying clothes and not hindering it, redirecting to the websites can keep the process fluent.

Sitemap

The structure of the app

I decided to keep just two main sections, as they're the primary actions of the app. The Home section will display mainly the search bar and some recommendations of clothes. The profile is a section that will be used to log in or update your information. The other main activity is adding an item, and I decided to give it one whole section.

User Flow

There are two main actions that a user can do in the app

The first one is trying to find your size of an item you want to buy, and the second one is adding an item to the app in order to help people to find the best size that fits them in an item you've bought.

Style Guide

An app for everyone

I decided to keep the app as simple as possible, giving the protagonism to the items.

This app will be used by men and women, the blue color is the most popular color for both, and it builds trust, something I want to accomplish with the app.

Almost all the e-commerce websites use a sans serif font, I decided to keep this trend using Lato, as their strong structure provides stability and seriousness.

In the test, I discovered new problems

I had the chance to do this test with 4 people, as the people who helped me with the test in the wireframes, they could navigate through the app without complication, but they still had some problems in certain sections.

  • This time they noticed the help icon, but nobody clicked it. I asked them why, and they told me that they thought it was going to display a small tooltip, so they preferred no to click it.
  • They didn't know that not all the fields in the registration form weren't obligatory.
  • They didn't understand the meaning of the scan icon, the one next to the search bar.
  • They liked the suggestions section, but they wanted some filters that would allow them to explore the items by price, brand, etc.
  • They wanted to know in which aspects other people had similar measures, in order to help them to make a decision.
  • They couldn't cancel and check all the information they wrote in the add item.

The Final Design

The design of the app

Onboarding

In my first test, I noticed that the users didn't get, at first glance, what was the purpose of the app. The onboarding shows the purpose of the app and what you can do in it.

Log In

Register

I separated the register form into four steps, as this process is long. Each step is focused on one goal, not all the fields are required, but the app can give better recommendations with the more information it has from its users.

Home

The home section allows users to see their personal information, search the size of an item they want to buy by its name or photo, and get recommendations generated by the app. These recommendations are based on the information the app has about the user, and this is going to be the main feature that will help to get funds in order to keep alive this project.

Search

The search has to be quick and really effective in order to improve the process of buying an item. I decided to add two ways to search, by photo and the traditional way (typing the name). Searching by photo is a feature I've seen in other apps, and I think it can improve the way of finding an item in the app, as it reduces as much as possible the time of searching an item.

Profile

The profile section will display the measures of the users, allowing them to update all this information. I decided no to collocate this section in the tab bar because this may not be used as constantly as the main sections.

Single Item

This is the section that accomplishes the main goal of the app. The recommendation is based on the information users provide about the item, it's obtained by calculating the average of all the measures users provided about each size and comparing the results with the measures of the user.

Based on the information the app has about each item, it displays recommendations of stores where users can buy what they're looking for, displaying at the top the best price. This is added because it helps people in the process of buying an item, but also because stores can realize the traffic that is generated from the app to its website, and fund the app in some way.

Single Item Comments

The comments will help users to know the experience other people had with the item, the ones at the top are from people with similar bodies, and users can view their profile if they want to know in which aspects they had similarities. This can be a privacy problem for some users, so I decided not to show neither the image nor the name of the ones that don't want to share that information.

Add Item

This section is the heart of the app, everything depends on what people do in this section, and while more information we get, better recommendations could be given.

The form is divided into three steps, the first one is to know general information about the item (name, brand, price, store), the second one is to know the real measures of the size, and the last one to know the experience users had bought the item. All this together will populate the database and will help to generate the recommendations.

Test

Testing one more time

Once I finished the final design and the prototype, I did a final test with 3 people. I tested the main actions in the app and realize that even though the results were pretty good, the app needed some improvements, for instance, the forms were too long and some people only filled the obligatory fields.

What I learned

Taking a real-life problem and ideating a solution

  • I need to improve my forms. The forms in the app took me so much time, and I know that I can do it better.
  • Testing early is awesome. I had the chance to do a test with the wireframes, and it made me realize some things that could be solved before the next test, and avoid these problems again.
  • Being close with the target users is so important. Fortunately, I had some target users close to me, so it was easier understanding their needs and problems.
