Design Challenge

Your school wants to provide dedicated spaces for students to help them focus on their academic studies. Design an experience for students, professors, and members of the school community to reserve work desks/spaces in the library

The Problem

Students and professors struggle to find spaces to focus on their studies because the ones at school don't fit their necessities

The library at school is a place where a lot of the school community go to study due to the commodity and resources they find there, but they sometimes face some problems that make them decide to go somewhere else.

Who am I designing for?

Students and teachers of the UNAM who want to find a place to focus on their academic studies

The survey done by Movilidad UNAM in June 2020 showed that 97% of students have a smartphone, and the university provides free access to the internet over the campuses, therefore, almost every member of the school community has access to this technology.

User Interviews

My goal was to understand how people currently use the library and identify areas of improvement

I interviewed remotely one student and one professor of the school community, through the conversation I explored the reasons why they use the library over other alternatives, their past experiences, and problems they've faced.

Professor's experience
  • The library is a great place to be closer to the students.
  • The tools in the library aren't enough when you go with a large group of people, a classroom with a board is a better option in this case.
  • Setting up a session with students is complicated because of the diffrent schedules, besides, it's not sure your are going to find an available space.
  • Students get distracted easily because of the noise and people in the group spaces.
Student's experience
  • The library provides resources that help you when you want to study alone or in groups.
  • Regularly the library is full, and finding available spaces is a nightmare, even though you want to use the individual rooms.
  • The environment changes depending on the rooms, individual rooms are quiet, on the other hand, group rooms are noisy.
  • As finding a place is hard, there are times when you spend more time than you planned and you ended up getting late for your classes.

Affinity Diagram

With all the information I collected, I created an Affinity Diagram to organize it into groups based on their relationship and develop new and deeper insights

I gathered different kinds of information, such as facts, needs, opinions, problems, and ideas. Through the development of the diagram, I found some patterns and relationships that helped me define problems and then develop potential solutions.

Research Insights

I found 2 major insights about the problem space

Although we believe that a library should be a quiet space, I uncovered that the community need special spaces to work in groups in the library

A lot of people use the library because of the resources and tranquility you find there, therefore, they are bothered when there are people talking. On the other hand, some people need a space where they can talk and have the same tranquility and access to the resources, without bothering other people.

The community believe that the way the library works just benefit people with a certain schedule, therefore, the chance to access these spaces isn't the same for everyone

Going to the library and finding out that it is full is something that almost everyone has experienced, they solve the problem by returning later or going another day earlier. Their schedule gets affected, and they have to make some sacrifices, but it turns out that not everyone experiences this problem due to their schedule is different from the rest of the students.

Sketches

I started ideating potential solutions and translating them into sketches, so I can explore different approaches quickly

I explored different solutions to help the community reserve spaces, such as creating an app, a big machine located at the door of the library, reservations machines located in different places of the campus, etc. Also, I ideated possible solutions to create spaces where people can work in groups, I thought about reorganizing the library, building new spaces, defining special hours, etc.

The Solution

UBook is a project that provides tools for desks and rooms connected with an app that lets people reserve the spaces anytime, anywhere

New Organization

With the new desks and spaces, the libraries should be reorganized

The organization of books in the library must be exceptional, so people can find what they want without problems. The same needs to happen with the new spaces, a good organization is going to make the experience of reserving and using the new spaces exceptional.

Why the whole library isn't full of UBook desks?

The main goal of UBook is to give anyone the chance to access space in the library. Approximately, 97% of students have a smartphone, and the internet is free inside the campus. However, the number of students is close to 360,883, therefore, the number of students without access to UBook desks is huge.

What if?

What are some problems people could face with UBook spaces?

What if nobody reserved a space and I want to use it?

UBook spaces will show the status of the spaces, available spaces can be used without reservations, but you'll have to leave it when someone with a reservation arrives.

What if I don't know which one is the space I reserved?

UBook spaces will have a unique number, and when you reserve one, the app will show you which one you reserved.

What if someone doesn't want to leave the space I reserved?

You can report any issue in the app, therefore, when you face any problem, the people in the library will help you.

What if a space is reserved, but the person that reserved it didn't show up?

People will have some time (10-15 minutes) to show up, if they don't arrive on time, space will change its status to available.

UBook Desk

How does the desk look like?

To use the desks already available in the library, I designed a tool that is going to be adapted to them. The device is going to show the information people need to know about a desk, its number, status, and the time it's status its going to change. The QR code is what is going to help us know if the person who reserved the space arrived.

UBook Room

How does the room look like?

Rooms are spaces where people are able to work in groups, having all the resources the library provides and the chance to be isolated from the rest, therefore, they are not getting distracted and don't distract, anyone.

The spaces include the same tool that the one in the desks, therefore, the interaction keeps the same. The transparent walls are going to help people join a room fast when they are late, as they are capable to know who is in each room. Furthermore, these walls are safer for the community, as people can see what's going on.

Design

Helping everyone to reserve spaces in the library

Log In

Use your institutional email to Log In or Register. Your security is important, that's why only members of the school community are going to have access to the app.

Home

The home screen introduces you to the app, it'll suggest you the best and closest spaces at the moment, but if you want to choose a specific one, go to the search box. With UBook you can reserve any space on the whole campus.

Search

Search any space you want, with the calendar, you can select the dates and time you are looking for, and UBook will show you the spaces that fit your necessities.

Reservation

Navigate through the different places available and explore their schedule. Once you find the perfect place, reserve it before someone else does.

Arrive at your space

Once you arrive at your desk or room, be sure you scan the QR code, therefore, we can know that the space will be occupied.

Enjoy

You arrive at the library without worrying about the availability of spaces, now you can start your study session. Don't worry about the time, we will alert you when it's over, therefore, you won't be late for your next class.

Report an issue

Any problem with your space?
Don't worry, we are here to help you. Report any problem you experienced and the library staff will help you.

What I learned

  • The short time makes you focus on what is more important for your process, therefore, you have to prioritize certain tasks.
  • During my research I had the chance to talk to people from different backgrounds, seeing their perspectives made me realize that people use tools in very different ways.
